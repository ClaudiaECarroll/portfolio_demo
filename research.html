Future Research Directions

Emerging Research

Expanding Machine Learning Applications in Literary Studies

Building on my current postdoctoral work, I plan to expand the application of machine learning methods to new areas of literary analysis. This includes developing more sophisticated models for understanding reader cognition and exploring how computational approaches can illuminate previously unexamined aspects of literary interpretation.

Future work will also investigate how these methods can be applied to contemporary literature and digital texts, expanding beyond the nineteenth-century focus of my current research to understand how reading patterns and literary interpretation evolve with new media forms.

Future Research Contemporary Literature Digital Texts Literary Evolution
Collaborative Development

Interdisciplinary Method Development

My experience at TRIADS has highlighted the importance of developing computational methods that are both technically sophisticated and humanistically meaningful. Future research will focus on creating methodological frameworks that can bridge the gap between computational and interpretive approaches to literature.

This work will involve continued collaboration with computer scientists, cognitive researchers, and literary scholars to develop tools and approaches that enhance rather than replace traditional humanistic inquiry while opening new avenues for literary analysis.
